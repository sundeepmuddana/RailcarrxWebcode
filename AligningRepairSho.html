Aligning Repair Shop Strategy to the North American Rail Fleet

Each year Railinc provides the rail industry a review of the North American rail fleet utilizing data from Umler. Repair shops could benefit from this information by aligning their repair strategy with trends in this data, by specializing in equipment repairs that are of high value to the industry.

As you may know, Umler is the system of record for data pertaining to equipment that travels across the North American rail network including all mechanical data.  Repair shops often access this data to receive mechanical information regarding equipment under repair.  They may also use the system to update mechanical data.  Umler is key to making the North American rail network run.

Dr. David Humphrey, Senior Analyst with Railinc, researches and presents statistics at the annual Rail Equipment Finance Conference in La Quinta, California.  The information paints a picture of the railcar and locomotive fleet demographics.  Some of the information presented is of little surprise, other data points may provide insight on where to focus your efforts as a repair shop in servicing specific equipment types.

Currently, Umler contains just over 2 million registered pieces of equipment, 2,060,914 to be precise.  This number was recorded during the fourth quarter of 2015 and represents a 0.2% increase since last recorded in the fourth quarter of 2014.  This is good news as the fleet is still growing. Furthermore, of the eleven equipment types reported in Umler the three largest segments are as follows:

Covered Hopper – 26%
Tank Cars – 21%
Gondolas – 12%

Interestingly, of these three segments, only two realized positive growth in total number of equipment, Covered Hoppers (0.8%) and Tank Cars (1.6%).  Gondolas saw a negative percentage change meaning that segment is shrinking (-1.9%).

The percentage of growth (or contraction) by segment across all 11 types of equipment is as follows:

Tanks +1.6%
Flats +1.0%
EOTs +0.9%
Covered Hoppers +0.8%
Locomotives +0.3%
Intermodal -0.2%
IM Flats -0.5%
Hoppers -0.6%
Misc. -1.1%
Box Cars -1.4%
Gondolas -1.9%

Another key piece of analysis the report reveals is revenue-earnings of the fleet.  According to the report, Tank Cars and Covered Hoppers helped drive growth in the revenue-earning fleet.  Box Cars and Hoppers shrank for the sixth consecutive year.

Covered Hoppers are the largest sub-fleet of the North American equipment pool sitting at 519,000 cars.  According to Railinc this segment has experienced growth of 2.7% or more in three of the the last four years.  Tank Cars on the other hand have shown the greatest increase of sub-fleets, however, growth rates have slowed.  In our opinion, this is most likely due to the downturn in North American fracking.

The question remains, if I am a repair shop, where should I focus my efforts to maximize revenues and gain more customers?  We certainly see many regulations that have increased work on Tank Cars, however, this may not be sustainable in the future.  As a repair shop, your best bet is to diversify, but at the same time focus on the top revenue producing segments of the market.  If Covered Hopper are producing significant revenue, you can bet that car owners and shippers will want repairs completed quickly and accurately to keep these revenue producers rolling.
