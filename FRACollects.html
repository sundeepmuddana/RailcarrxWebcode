“FRA Collects Highest Civil Penalty Rate for Rail Safety Violations” Is it Time To Panic?

Did you catch this recent headline “FRA collects highest civil penalty rate for rail safety violations¹” from Progressive Railroading that was published on January 20th, 2016? We did.  It isn’t shocking that the FRA is increasing the amount of penalties they are issuing and collecting. With the series of events that have occurred and negative press around them, it appears (at least in our opinion) that the FRA is probably reacting in part to public opinion that the rails are unsafe.

The article states the following statistics regarding the rise in civil penalties:

  1. “In 2015 the agency will collect 75 percent of all civil penalties for violating federal safety regulations, a 6 PERCENT INCREASE over FY2014″
  2. “The agency collected $15 million in FY2015, a 12 PERCENT INCREASE over the previous year”

The article goes on to say that more than 6,485 railroad company violations resulted in civil penalties. The penalties are broken down below:

  1. 29% was for motive power and equipment violations
  2. 26% was for track violations
  3. 20% was for HAZMAT violations

If you want to see how these fines are broken down read the FRA’s report “ Fiscal Year 2015 Enforcement Report.”

As a repair shop, this likely affects you.  If you have not had a visit from the FRA, it may just be a matter of time.

As a repair shop, what can you do to improve your preparedness should the FRA show up at your location?  Here are a few tips, and how RailcarRx Repair Shop can help improve your odds of being prepared.

  1. Have a Plan – Our customers have told us that the best way to prepare for an FRA inspection is to have an advance plan.  This plan would include all your paperwork and documentation should an inspection occur.  The documentation (according to our customers) that you should have includes:
    1. Quality Assurance Manual
    2. C3 Manual
    3. Procedures forms
    4. Equipment calibration logs (more information below)
    5. Equipment data files (more information below)
    6. AAR Field Manual
  2. Practice Your Plan – The best way to make sure that your plan works is to practice a drill where an FRA inspector has shown up and is requesting specific information.
  3. Be Nice If the FRA Shows Up – Cooperating with the FRA inspector is the best way to get good results. Being friendly and helpful goes a long way. Keep in mind that you only need to show what information the FRA requests. Don’t give away extraneous information.

More on section 1, points #4 & #5.  One of our customers told us they keep a file of ten cars with supporting information for this purpose, in case an FRA inspector unexpectedly shows up. All the information they would need to provide is contained within the Electronic Car File in RailcarRx Repair Shop.  The data in the file includes all the paperwork from arrival through invoicing for those pieces of equipment.  RailcarRx Repair Shop captures all this data electronically and stores it for all equipment that has already been repaired or is currently under repair in your facility. We provide the ability to upload customer emails, MSDS sheets, images, car owner specs, forms, and any other information that may be required for you to provide to the FRA and attach it to the equipment in the Electronic Car File (see screen shot below).  Additionally, RailcarRx Repair Shop provides the ability to print these documents in bulk if an inspector should arrive onsite.

RailcarRx Repair Shop allows users to add files to a piece of equipment through the Electronic Car File and store them for historical reporting needs. RailcarRx Repair Shop also provide the ability to store and manage gage calibration data. Why is the helpful? Storing information in one system is better than having it spread out in many locations. RailcarRx Repair Shop provides notifications to users when gage calibrations are due, which can help you prevent issues should an FRA inspection take place. In the words of Alfred E. Neuman, “What…Me Worry?”  If you are prepared and have a plan in place in the event of an FRA inspection, you can put the panic and worry behind you. Arming yourself with the proper tools to help increase your odds of a smooth experience (such as using RailcarRx Repair Shop) will only help decrease your odds of getting stuck in a bind.
